    Game Title: 3 O'Clock Horror

    About the game: 3 O’clock horror is a short escape the room inspired experience that takes a lot of inspiration from other horror games like Resident Evil and Faith, as well as Lovecraftian tales to make a single short horror experience that can be easily replayed to achieve different endings.
    This game has a total of 3 endings and full a Mansion to explore with 3 enemies that will “wake up” at different times during the players' exploration of the Mansion. Like other horror games, some of the story is told through notes and messages left behind in the Mansion walls.

    What I did:

    • Led a team of 9 students through the design of the game
    • Designed puzzles and the level design flow of the game
    • Designed the background art for all the rooms and a few items for puzzles and decoration
    • Wrote and fine tuned the story behind the game and restructured the way the protagonist tells the story based on events happening in the game
    • Completely built and released the game on Steam as a free-to-play title
    • Optimized the game for android phones and re-designed UI for touchscreen

    Links: Development Process | Steam Page

    Platforms: PC, Android

    Project Length: 6 months (PC), additional 3 months (Android)

    Engine, Tools, and Language: Unity, Adobe Suite, GitHub, Trello, C# Visual Studio

    Game Title: Stresstris

    Link: Development Process | Itch.io Page

    Platforms: PC

    Project Length: 3 months

    Engine, Tools, and Language: Unity, Adobe Suite, Blender, C# Visual Studio

    About the game: This game is a short experience that mixes platformers and Tetris. It allows the path to the top of the map to be unique every time the game is played because all the blocks are randomly selected and randomly placed to fall on the grid.
    The game ramps up the difficulty with stop points that help clear the screen, and events that will slowly delete the floor until only the Tetris blocks that fell remain. To help the player we have 2 power ups that can be used during the game, one making you faster and the other allowing you to use a second jump.

    What I did:

    • Led and design the project with the team
    • delegate meaningful tasks that would help the project finish in time while keeping the intended experience
    • Design mechanics, powerups, and level design challenges for the player to experience during the level
    • Help with debugging the 3D blocks, their spawn point and delete areas
    • Design and implementation of the title screen of the game, as well as menu options during gameplay

    Game Title: Antidote

    About the game: Antidote is a cure finding game where the player is a scientist trying to manufacture a cure for a pathogen. The game mechanics involves the player trying different stats in the lab and taking notes on what is most effective to kill the pathogen without killing too many neutral cells.

    What I did:

    • Programmed cell behaviors for pathogen, neutral, and modified cells that the player can control
    • Added the flocking for the cells to walk together as a pack of cells and attack together and reproduce
    • Created a selecting menu for doctors that can help the player find the cure
    • Implemented art and animation on Unity
    • Helped design the choose mechanic of the game and link the results with the modifiable cells

    Link: Development Process

    Platforms: PC

    Project Length: 5 months

    Engine, Tools, and Language: Unity, Github, C# Visual Studio
